Pandemic Productivity... another comics!

While the 1st page was published on March 29th, it was not until April last week that an actual story was formulated.

But it took lot more than what I had anticipated to complete the art. It was not until July second week that I was able to release this.

Fair warning, comics are best experienced on a laptop or desktop.

Few images taken out while making.

As with everything I made, after sometime mistakes started to glare out at me from the pages of An Agent. After a lot of deliberation, I finally decided to fix some of them. I did not fix everything, rather the ones which I felt made most impact.

The major one was page 12. It felt rushed. Lot of things happenned on a single page. This triggered a weird arrangement of panels. The haphazard panel arrangement in turn caused the important frames not getting enough space to convey the narrative.

  • The frames shaded red are the one that I felt should have been given much more space to convey the narrative.
  • The frame shaded in green was not so important to take up so much space on this page. It was done as a filler on this page.

Page 12 was split into 2 pages (12A and 12B) with updated art. It was here that I hated myself for putting page numbers directly on the art.

The next was page 17. I didn't like the way the reader was lead to the flow of the page. This is the bottom half of the page that I felt was problematic.

This was a quick fix though. I switched the art horizontally. Did the same to the upper half to balance the space all around.
