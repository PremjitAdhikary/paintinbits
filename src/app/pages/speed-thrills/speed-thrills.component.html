After working on a action comic, I wanted to try my hand on depicting motion. Need for Speed it is!

I have been working on this story for almost two years now. I was struggling to come up with a proper ending. I had stopped working on this mid-way to work on other projects, just so that I could come back to this with a fresh perspective. One such project was inspired by this story. Read it here.

Fair warning, comics are best experienced on a laptop or desktop.

This time, the goal was to draw motion and detailed backgrounds, landscape to be precise. Like my previous attempt, I kept my focus just on that. Racing cars in motion through mountains. No character design required! I also did not want to overcomplicate by drawing different car models, so one model with different colors to differentiate the players.

Boundaries defined, time to come up with a script. Right, it should be easy, the race starts with a bunch of racers, and we end up with a winner. Where's the drama? Let me tell you, this was a hard script to finalise. About half-way through I just stopped (was this writer's block?). I started on the artwork instead. A very risky thing to do - I had already burnt my hands before in a previous project where I had to redo a lot of art just because I changed the script. Fortunately I had other projects to work on to distract me.

Did I mention it took almost two years for me to complete this project? Unfinished script was the primary reason. I came up with a proper ending sometime in July 2025. Finalised the script in August. Then back to the art.

The other reason for two years? The artstyle I selected for comics. I went for Pseudo Realistic Art. This is a style that I take my own sweet time to realise - and I can still see a lot of room for improvement.
