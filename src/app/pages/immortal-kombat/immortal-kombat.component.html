For this one I risked an attempt on creating a fast-paced-action comics. Almost immediately the game Mortal Kombat came to mind.

Read on for a No Holds Barred all out FIGHT.

Fair warning, comics are best experienced on a laptop or desktop.

My primary drive to create this comics was to try my hands at drawing action. For that I kept the number of characters to a minimum of two (you need at least 2 fighters to fight). Also I did not want to stress on a lot for backgrounds. So that was kept simple. A room in a monastery where the fighters fight.

With the boudaries defined, I came up with a rough script. I kept the script loose so that while I worked on art, based on what felt/looked good, I could re-arrange the flow of the story.

My process can be summarised as:

  1. Based on rough script, do a rough story board
  2. Based on story board, find references. Mortal Kombat is a popular game and the internet was a treasure trove of resources for me to draw my references from. The references helped finalise the story board.
  3. With the finalised story board in hand, all was left was to art it out

The other aspect that I experimented with is on how to depict dynamism in my art. While the game itself displays the fighters from a side view, which of course works for the game, here I had an opportunity to try other angles. The basic idea was to find a perspective which would present the scene with the biggest impact.
